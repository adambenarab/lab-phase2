TOURISM PLACES

Discover the best tourism places in Tunisia

Tozeur

Tozeur, the largest of the oases and the most beautiful with its superb palm grove! Take the time to stroll through the narrow streets of the Medina with its brick houses with beautiful patterns and typical studded doors. You will surely observe women there weaving beautiful carpets. Located at the gates of the desert, Tozeur will be a good starting point for a camel or 4x4 ride.

get location

Carthage

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Carthage was once a very important commercial city. Built on a hill overlooking the Gulf of Tunis, the site contains vestiges testifying to more than 2,000 years of history. Over the years, several civilizations have succeeded each other, each of them leaving their mark on the various monuments of the city. Start your visit on the hill in Byrsa with the museum displaying archaeological objects from the site.

get location

Douz, porte d'entrée du Sahara

In southern Tunisia, the oasis of Douz is renowned for being the "gateway to the Sahara", the starting point for excursions in the desert. Surrounded by sand dunes, the city retains its nomadic traditions and captivates with its warm and atypical atmosphere. Take advantage of the setting to treat yourself to an excursion by 4x4, on foot… or by camel!


get location

Chott el-Djérid

Located between two key cities in southern Tunisia: Tozeur and Kébili, the Chott el-Djérid is a salt lake of around 7000 m², an unusual almost lunar landscape! Excursions are organized there and are generally done aboard a 4x4. It is a unique and relaxing place ideal for a day off the beaten track!

Ksar-Ghilane

Dougga is an archaeological site, a hundred kilometers from Tunis, in excellent condition and probably one of the best preserved in the whole of the Maghreb. Classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, its remains are located on a hill and extend over more than 25 hectares. It takes half a day to observe most of the buildings such as the theater, the capitol, the thermal baths, several temples and a mausoleum...

El Jem

The El Jem amphitheater is located in the small town of the same name in the Sahel region about 30 km from the city of Mahdia. It is the largest colosseum in North Africa and has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979. This work was built in 238 AD, an architectural feat for the time!



get location

Sidi Bou Saïd

Right next to the famous city of Carthage and its thermal baths, the charming village of Sidi Bou Saïd stands out for its architecture and its houses colored in blue and white. Stroll through the small cobbled streets of this village and admire the doors of the houses, which are, for the most part, delicately decorated. You will find in this charming village many hotels and guest houses, which facing the sea will offer their visitors landscapes of rare beauty.
